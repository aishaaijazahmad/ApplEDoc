Class: MoralIntention

Annotations (1)

  • rdfs:comment "Moral Intention is defined as the ability to prioritise moral values over other values and hence requires that the desired outcome is bound by moral values.

    (From A. C. Ping, Strategic Ethical Consultancy).

    Moral intent is the desire to act ethically when facing a decision and overcome the rationalization to not be ethical “this time.” Even if a person sees the ethical aspects of a decision and has the philosophical tools to make the right choice, he or she still needs to want to do the right thing. If a person has no moral intent, then moral awareness and moral decision making are useless.

    (From BEING YOUR BEST SELF, PART 3: MORAL INTENT, Ethics Unwrapped.)"     @en
