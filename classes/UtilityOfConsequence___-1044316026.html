Class: UtilityOfConsequence

Annotations (1)

  • rdfs:comment "The utility of consequence describes the affect of the action from a good and bad perspective. It is also important to note that some consequences are neither good nor bad, they are neutral. A stricter view states that all actions have some good and/or bad consequences. In an action where no consequence has occured, the action itself was futile, due to which it was a waste of energy for the agent, thus a bad consequence." @en
